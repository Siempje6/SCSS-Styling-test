Open Intelligence in Practice: Building Transparent AI Systems

In our previous post, we shared how we’re building frontier open intelligence — a new generation of open models forming the foundation for transparent, accessible, and powerful AI. Today, we’re taking the next step: how do we turn that vision into practice? We believe open intelligence is not just a principle — it’s a way of building. It means research, data, and model performance should be understandable, inspectable, and improvable by anyone who wants to participate.

20 October 2025



The Power of Transparency

When models are shared openly, a new kind of collaboration emerges. Universities can validate results, startups can innovate faster, and independent researchers can identify bias before it causes harm. Transparency isn’t just an ethical stance — it’s a technological catalyst. By publishing open benchmarks and model cards, we enable objective comparisons and accelerate innovation across the AI ecosystem.

Our Approach: Measuring What Matters

We’ve made our internal evaluation methods public to show how open models perform on reasoning, comprehension, and safety.

Below is a simplified snapshot of recent results:

Model Name Parameters Benchmark (Reasoning) Bias Score Released
Reflection-Base 12B 83% 0.31 ↓ Yes
Reflection-Pro 40B 89% 0.27 ↓ Yes
Reflection-X 120B 93% 0.22 ↓ In progress


The Bias Score represents the degree of subjective bias (lower is better). Our ongoing goal is to develop models that are not only powerful but also aligned and responsible.

Learning From the Community

The open-source community has proven that collaboration accelerates progress. Every pull request, dataset review, and research paper adds to our collective understanding. That’s why we actively collaborate with universities and independent labs to improve our reinforcement learning pipeline — allowing us to train on more diverse data while continuously reducing bias.

A Glimpse Into the Future

Over the last year, we've been preparing for this mission.In the coming months, we’ll release a visual update to our model dashboard: an interactive platform for comparing performance, submitting feedback, and running custom experiments. We’re also working on the next generation of agentic reasoning models — systems capable of not only predicting but also planning, reflecting, and learning from their own mistakes.

Closing Thoughts

Open intelligence is not a finish line — it’s a journey we walk together. By building in the open, we can ensure that the foundation of intelligence remains transparent, trustworthy, and shared by all.
